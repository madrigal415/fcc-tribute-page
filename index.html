Herbert Needleman, MD

The intrepid doctor whose studies revealed the harm caused by lead exposure

This guy spent his life caring for children. He treated children, and time and again he found himself caring for children poisoned by lead. Recognizing the problem, he became one of the first to study the effects from low-level exposures. His study examined lead in children's teeth and found that higher levels were linked to lower IQ scores. His research contributed to regulations that removed lead from gasoline and paint. After these regulations went into place lead levels plummeted.

History of Herb

  • 1950 Graduated Medical School
  • 1972 Herb publishes study linking high levels of lead in teeth in children to lower IQ
  • 1979 Herb publishes major study in a population of suburban chldren finding that low-levels effects are real
  • 1991 CDC proposes near universal testing
  • 1994 AAP comes out against universal testing.
  • 1997 CDC releases guidelines that recommend testing in some children
  • 1998 Herb publishes article railing against the failure to follow through with universal testing
  • 2004 Herb publishes article on Lead Poisoning for Annual Review of Medicine that outlines his major work and the state of the field
